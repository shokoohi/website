Markov chain simulator

2014

It is often difficult to see what happens in long interations if you move from a state to another state with some probability. Simulate your Markov chain here and see what happens in the long run.

  • Mia Godet
  • Ylan Luu
  • David Binh Quang Tran

Buffon 's needle

2014

We have a floor made of parallel strips of wood, each the same width, and we drop a needle onto the floor. What is the probability that the needle will lie across a line between two strips?

  • Jules Thuillier
  • Erwan Marchand

Normal distribution

2014

Normal distribution plays a central role statistics because many summary statistics follow the normal distribution for a large sample size. Simulate binary variables here and track the empirical distribution of their mean.

  • Alexandre Choinière
  • Rodolphe Brossard
  • Julien Greschner

The Birthday Paradox

2014

In the probability theory, the birthday paradox is about the number of people that we have to group together to have a 50% chance that two people in this group are going to have their birthday at the same date. You will be surprised to see that with small number of people, this probability is actually so high!

  • Félix La Rocque Carrier

M/M/1 Queue for 50 people

2014

In queueing theory, a discipline within the mathematical theory of probability, an M/M/1 queue represents the queue length in a system having a single server, where arrivals are determined by a Poisson process and the service time follows an exponential distribution.

  • Maxime Gauthier-Farley
  • Benoît Paradis
  • Nicolas Montreuil

Pi approximation using a circle

2014

The value of Pi is the proportional to the points uniformly distributed on the square and fall inside a circle.

  • Alexandre Arsenault
  • Aniss Kouyess

Monty Hall

2014

The Monty Hall problem is a brain teaser, in the form of a probability puzzle, loosely based on the American television game show "Let's Make a Deal" and named after its original host, Monty Hall.

  • Jean-François Richard-Leblanc
  • Philippe Groarke

Non-transitive Dice

2013

A set of unusual dice and a two-player game in which you will always have the advantage. You can even teach your opponent how the game works, yet still win again!
